# Privacy Policy for GCal Freetime Scraper

**Last Updated: April 2026**

This Privacy Policy describes how the **GCal Freetime Scraper** ("the Extension") handles user data. As a tool built by engineers, transparency and data sovereignty are our core principles.

## 1. Data Collection and Usage
The Extension requests access to your Google Calendar data via the Google Calendar API. This access is used **exclusively** to identify "Busy" blocks on your calendar to calculate your remaining free time.

* **Calendar Data:** Read-only access to your primary calendar events and settings.
* **Processing:** All calculations are performed locally within your browser's execution environment. No personal data is sent to the developer or any third party.

## 2. Data Storage and Transmission
The Extension follows a strict "No-Server" architecture:

* **No External Storage:** Your calendar data, events, and free-time summaries are never stored on any external database or server.
* **No Third-Party Sharing:** No data is ever shared with, sold to, or tracked by third-party services.
* **Clipboard:** Data is only placed on your system clipboard at your explicit request.

## 3. Permissions Explained
The Extension requires the following permissions to function:

* **identity:** To authenticate with your Google account and retrieve an OAuth2 token.
* **calendar.readonly:** To view your busy slots.
* **contextMenus:** To provide the right-click "Copy Freetime" option.

## 4. Third-Party Services
The Extension interacts with Google APIs. Your use of Google services is governed by the [Google Privacy Policy](https://policies.google.com/privacy).

## 5. Contact
If you have questions regarding this policy, you may contact the developer via the GitHub repository or the Chrome Web Store support link.
